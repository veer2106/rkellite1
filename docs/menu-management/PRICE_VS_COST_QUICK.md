# Quick Answer: Price vs Cost

## They Are DIFFERENT! 🎯

### **PRICE** = What Customers Pay 💵
- **Shown in POS** - Customers see this
- **Used for billing** - Amount charged
- **Example:** Pancakes for ₹250
- **Required:** ✅ YES

### **COST** = What It Costs You to Make 🛒
- **Hidden from customers** - Internal only
- **Used for profit tracking** - Management reports
- **Example:** Ingredients cost ₹80
- **Required:** ❌ NO (Optional)

---

## Real Example:

```
Making Coffee:
───────────────────────────────
Cost (your expense):
- Coffee beans: ₹15
- Milk: ₹10
- Sugar/labor: ₹5
─────────────
COST = ₹30  ← Enter in "Cost" field

Price (customer pays):
- Selling price: ₹100  ← Enter in "Price" field

Your Profit:
₹100 - ₹30 = ₹70 profit per cup! 💰
```

---

## In the Menu Form:

```
Price: ₹100    ← What customer pays (REQUIRED)
Cost:  ₹30     ← Your expense (OPTIONAL but helpful)
```

**Profit = Price - Cost**

---

## Why Fill Both?

✅ Track which items are most profitable  
✅ Make smarter pricing decisions  
✅ Know if you're losing money on items  
✅ See profit reports in dashboard
